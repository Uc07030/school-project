Costs Associated with the First Year at the University of Nigeria, Nsukka (UNN)

Embarking on the academic journey at the University of Nigeria Nsukka entails significant financial responsibilities for recently admitted students. The transition from clearance procedures to various payments may pose a financial challenge for parents and guardians.

First and foremost, congratulations to the new students (freshmen) for securing admission to one of the most competitive universities. A warm welcome is extended to you.

Securing admission to UNN involves various financial obligations, including acceptance fees, school fees, accommodation charges, and additional expenses. It is essential for first-year students to comprehend the breakdown of these costs to effectively plan their budget and communicate with their parents or guardians.

This post offers insights into the different expense categories that UNN freshmen can anticipate. Both parents and newly admitted students at the UNN or UNEC Campus will find this information valuable in preparing for the financial aspects of university life.

Explanation of the Above Listed Fees

Acceptance fee is the amount of money you pay to accept your UNN admission. It is the first payment you will make as a student of the University of Nigeria, Nsukka.

The amount for the UNN acceptance fee is thirty thousand naira (₦30,000).

Do not pay the acceptance fee using your ATM card. All payments must be at any of the commercial banks in Nigeria. The reason is that the bank teller would be a requirement for your final year clearance.

School Fees School fees are the amount of money you would pay for different charges like ICT, SUG, development fee, etc.

UNN school fees for freshers is between seventy to eighty thousand naira (₦70,000 – 80,000).

Note: UNN school fees changes for every academic session.

Read this post to see the school fees schedule for all the departments in UNN.

Also, payment for the UNN school fees must be at the commercial bank. Do not make payment with your ATM card.

UNN Clearance Fee The UNN clearance is free of charge.

You do not pay any money for clearance as a student of the University of Nigeria.

There are lots of expenses you will make during your clearance. They include the following;

Passport Photograph You need thirty (30) passports for your academic and hostel clearance.

Also, you will use passports for your manual course registration in UNN.

Photocopy of Documents You need four (4) copies of each of your clearance documents. That is a lot of money.

I will advise you not to make any clearance expenses at home. Proceed to the university first; there may be some changes to the charges. It is more accurate to verify your clearance payment so as not to make any wrong payment.

UNN School Hostel and Accommodation Information

UNN School Hostel: The UNN school hostel charges are twelve thousand naira for male students and fifteen thousand naira for female students.

Off-campus Accommodation: Off-campus accommodation costs between forty to two hundred and fifty thousand naira for a single room and self-contained.

You should read my post on UNN Hostel and Off-campus accommodation. The article contains everything there is to know about accommodation in UNN.

UNEC students should read Hostel and Off-Campus accommodation in UNEC.

GS Textbooks & CA: GS stands for general studies, a compulsory course for all students at the University of Nigeria. First-year students offer between two (2) to three (3) GS courses for each semester in their 100 level. The GS textbook and CA are compulsory, costing between one thousand five hundred naira (₦1,500) to two thousand five hundred (₦2,500).

CA Booklet: CA stands for continuous assessment. Students of the College of Medicine, Pharmacy, Biological Sciences, Veterinary Medicine, and Physical Science buy CA booklets. There is a CA booklet for every course, costing between one to two thousand naira (₦1,000 – 2000).

Practical Manuals: Practical manuals are for first science students. The cost for these practical manuals (PHY 191, CHM 171, BIO 154, ZEB) is between one five to three thousand five hundred naira (₦1,500 – 3,500).

Dissecting Kit: Dissecting kits are instruments for BIO 154 practicals for science students, costing three thousand five hundred naira (₦3,500).

Photocopy Materials: In UNN, there are plenty of study materials and documents you will photocopy. There is no fixed price on the number of documents you will duplicate. The frequency at which you would photocopy materials would depend on your department.

Pass Questions: Pass questions are necessary for you to pass your semester exams in UNN.

Additional University Expenses Information

You can spend up to five thousand naira (₦5,000) to buy past questions and answers.

But, you would not spend any money if you decide to get past questions in soft copies.

Textbooks: Buying textbooks is not compulsory in most departments in UNN. You can meet your senior colleagues to get their textbooks to save costs or get them in soft copies. But if otherwise, you would spend more than ten thousand naira (₦5,000) per semester getting all the textbooks.

Faculty Dues & Departmental Dues: Faculty and departmental dues are the money you will pay to your faculty and department. Both dues can be up to ten thousand naira (₦10,000).

Class Dues: Class due is the money you will pay to your class representative after the start of lectures. The money is for the running of the class expenses like printing of class list, etc. Class dues in UNN are five hundred naira (₦500) or one thousand naira (₦1,000), depending on the decision of your class.

Manual Course Registration: Manual registration is registering your course for the semester with the various departments. Manual course registration in UNN is one thousand naira (₦1,000).
