Things You Should Know about UNN

As you may be aware, UNN is currently ranked as one of the best universities, and they are stringent in the admission process to identify the best students using their post-UTME. read more

Now be informed: The University of Nigeria, Nsukka (UNN) does not accept second-choice candidates for admission, whether for UTME admission or Direct Entry admission.

Any aspirant choosing UNN as a second-choice university is wasting their time. The university's management has consistently communicated this through the university’s website and during the sale of their post-UTME forms, emphasizing that admission is only for candidates who have selected UNN as their first-choice institution.

Often, the university may not even offer admission to candidates with low UTME scores due to the high competition for slots in the school.

For those wondering what to do if they have chosen UNN as their second-choice institution, there's good news. Apply for JAMB change of course and institution form for just N2,500. This will allow you to change your institution or course.

Make sure to apply for this form as early as possible, as there is a deadline for the application. This is the only alternative solution for you if you have already designated UNN as your second-choice institution.
