DEPARTMENT OF BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

The Department of Business Administration has undergone significant transformations over the years, evolving from the Department of Management Studies to the Department of Accounting and Management Sciences and finally to its current status as the Department of Business Administration. The separation of the Departments of Accounting and Management Sciences was formally recognized by the University of Jos Senate in 2004. Post-separation, the Department was initially named Management Sciences.

Admission Requirements for B.Sc. Business Management:

Candidates must possess a minimum of 5 credits at O’Level, including English Language, Mathematics, Economics/Commerce, and any other two subjects in Business studies, Government, Principles of accounts, Geography, and Biology. The JAMB candidates must obtain the cut-off points in JAMB from the four subjects: Use of English and any three relevant subjects.

Direct Entry Requirements (Three-Year Degree Programmes):

Candidates with IJMB/Advance Level GCE (A/L) should have at least two principal and one subsidiary passes in relevant subjects. Diploma level candidates must have completed from a recognized institution with a minimum of lower credit in related fields of study.

National Certificate in Education (NCE) Requirement:

Candidates with a merit pass in Business Education, Mathematics/Economics, Geography/Economics Secretarial Studies, and Financial Studies are eligible.

Professional Certificate Requirements:

Candidates passing professional courses like ICAN, CIB, ACIS, CIS, ACCA, NIM, and CMA or ICPA are eligible for admission.

Admission Requirements for Postgraduate Diploma (PGDM):

The PGDM program requires five credit passes, including English and Mathematics at the 'O' level. Bachelor's degrees with at least second class lower division or third class degree holders in Management-related areas are eligible.

Admission Requirements for MBA Program:

Candidates must have a Bachelors degree in Management, Accounting, Marketing, Business Administration, Banking and Finance, Actuarial Science, or any acceptable discipline. A minimum of one year managerial/administrative experience is required. Candidates with a postgraduate diploma from a recognized university may also be considered.

Duration of MBA Program:

The full-time MBA program runs for a minimum of four semesters and a maximum of six semesters, while the part-time program runs for a minimum of six semesters and a maximum of ten semesters. For extension beyond the maximum period, a special permission of the senate shall be required.

Admission Requirements for M.Sc Program:

Candidates must have 5 credit passes, including English, Mathematics, and Economics/Commerce at 'O' level. Bachelors degree holders with a minimum of second class (lower) in Business Admin/Management are eligible.

Admission Requirements for Ph.D Program:

Admission is open to graduates with a good Master of Science degree in Management/Business Administration. The duration varies for full-time (6 to 10 semesters) and part-time (10 to 12 semesters). For extension beyond the specified maximum period, special permission from the Postgraduate School shall be required.

General Requirements for Admission:

  • Candidates must obtain a minimum of five (5) credit passes in WAEC/SSCE/NECO OR GCE ‘O’ levels.
  • Subjects include English Language, Mathematics, Geography, and any two from Physics, Chemistry, Technical Drawing, Fine Arts, Economics, and Biology.
  • An acceptable pass in the Matriculation Examination (JME) is required.

Remedial Studies Admission (100 Level):

Pass the Remedial Studies Examination of the University of Jos in relevant subjects, including Geography, Mathematics, English Language, and Technical Drawing.

Direct Entry Admission (200 Level):

  • Candidates with National Diploma (ND) in related fields with an upper credit (e.g., Town Planning, Land Surveying, Building, Architecture, Estate Management, Quantity Survey) can be admitted.
  • At least two (2) passes at the ‘A’ level HSC or IJMB, with Geography as one of the subjects.

Program Duration:

The program spans five years or ten (10) semesters, leading to a degree in Urban and Regional Planning (B.URP). Direct entry students join at 200 level, with one semester dedicated to industrial attachment during the second semester of the 4th year.

Admission and Program Guidelines for Urban and Regional Planning:

General Requirements for Admission:

For 100 Level Admission:

  1. Candidates must obtain a minimum of five (5) credit passes in WAEC/SSCE/NECO OR GCE ‘O’ levels.
  2. Subjects include English Language, Mathematics, Geography, and any two from Physics, Chemistry, Technical Drawing, Fine Arts, Economics, and Biology.
  3. An acceptable pass in the Matriculation Examination (JME) is required.

Remedial Studies Admission (100 Level):

  • Pass the Remedial Studies Examination of the University of Jos in relevant subjects, including Geography, Mathematics, English Language, and Technical Drawing.

Direct Entry Admission (200 Level):

  • Candidates with National Diploma (ND) in related fields with an upper credit (e.g., Town Planning, Land Surveying, Building, Architecture, Estate Management, Quantity Survey) can be admitted.
  • At least two (2) passes at the ‘A’ level HSC or IJMB, with Geography as one of the subjects.

Program Duration:

  • The program spans five years or ten (10) semesters, leading to a degree in Urban and Regional Planning (B.URP).
  • Direct entry students join at 200 level, with one semester dedicated to industrial attachment during the second semester of the 4th year.
Guidelines for Course Selection and Registration:
  1. To qualify for a Bachelor in Urban and Regional Planning (B.URP), students must accumulate a minimum of 180 credit units (for 100 level) or 132 credit units (for 200 level direct entry).
  2. In any session, students can register a minimum of 36 and a maximum of 48 credits.
  3. Some courses have prerequisites, and only those who have satisfied the stipulated requirements can register for such courses.
Graduation Requirements:

Students must satisfy general and specific requirements for core/compulsory, optional, elective courses, and practicals.

Degree classification is based on the Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) system.

Class of Degree (Based on 5-point scale) CGPA:

First Class Honours: 4.50 – 5.00

Second Class Honours (Upper Division): 3.50 – 4.49

Second Class Honours (Lower Division): 2.40 – 3.49

Third Class Honours: 1.50 – 2.39

Fail: Below 1.50

Probation:

A student shall be placed on academic probation if at the end of the second semester of an academic year the student scored less than 1.0 Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA).

Withdrawal:

A student shall be requested to withdraw from a particular programme if at the end of a probation period, the student still does not make satisfactory progress. Such student shall be at liberty to apply for a change of programme within the university.
