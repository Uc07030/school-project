FACULTY OF ENGINEERING OVERVIEW

The Faculty of Engineering and Environmental Design (FEED) gained approval from the National University Commission (NUC) to initiate four academic programs in the 2016/2017 academic session, including Electrical-Electronic Engineering, Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Environmental Resource Management. Subsequently, in the 2018/2019 academic session, NUC granted approval for the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) degree program.

Departments:

Its primary goal is to provide extensive learning opportunities, producing civil engineers with proven engineering abilities. The department offers a 5-year Bachelor of Engineering (B.Eng.) DEPARTMENT OF MECHANICAL ENGINEERING OVERVIEW

The Department of Mechanical Engineering focuses on creating, integrating, transferring, and applying knowledge in the field. Offering an undergraduate program leading to a Bachelor of Engineering in Mechanical Engineering (B.Eng.), the curriculum equips students with the skills necessary for professional practice.

DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION AND COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY OVERVIEW

The Department of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) was slated to begin in the 2016/2017 Academic Session within the Faculty of Engineering and Environmental Design. However, NUC deferred its approval for inclusion in the faculty.

DEPARTMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL AND RESOURCES MANAGEMENT IN BRIEF

The Department of Environmental and Resources Management is one of the five foundation departments of the Faculty of Engineering and Environmental Design, established in 2016. Offering a five-year single honor (BSc) degree in Environmental and Resource Management, the department plays a pivotal role within the faculty.

DEPARTMENT OF SCIENCE AND VOCATIONAL EDUCATION IN BRIEF

The Department of Science and Vocational Education, originating from the Faculty of Education and Extension Services, has been an integral part of the university since its establishment in 1977. Over the years, it has evolved, offering a range of full-time and part-time undergraduate degree programs, as well as postgraduate programs in science and mathematics education.

DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATIONAL AND FOUNDATIONS IN BRIEF

The Department of Educational Foundations emerged from the Department of Education and has been a significant academic unit since 1977. Admitting students with O-levels and UME qualifications for a four-year program, and NCE/A-Level qualifications for a three-year B.A.Ed program, the department has expanded to offer a variety of postgraduate programs in education.

DEPARTMENT OF ARABIC STUDIES IN BRIEF

The Department of Arabic Studies, established in 1976, has played a crucial role in offering a B.A program since its inception. Initially, a three-year program, it later adapted to the 6-3-3-4 Education system. Over the years, the department has grown in both student enrollment and academic staff.

DEPARTMENT OF ISLAMIC STUDIES IN BRIEF

The Department of Islamic Studies, a part of the Faculty of Arts and Islamic Studies, was established in the 1976/77 academic session. With a dedicated teaching staff, the department has evolved to offer programs in Arabic, Arabic Literary Studies, Islamic Studies, and Quran and Hadith Studies.

DEPARTMENT OF ADULT EDUCATION AND EXTENSION SERVICES IN BRIEF

Founded in October 1977, the Department of Adult Education and Extension Services initially provided tertiary non-degree courses. Over time, it transitioned to offering degree programs and changed its name. The department now provides undergraduate, masters, and Ph.D. degree programs in Adult and Primary Education.

DEPARTMENT OF CURRICULUM STUDIES AND EDUCATIONAL TECHNOLOGY IN BRIEF

The Department, initially a unit under the 'Department of Education,' was established in October 1977 with 35 students. It became a full-fledged Department in 2009, named the Department of Curriculum Studies and Educational Technology. The department has evolved its academic system and currently offers programs such as B.Sc. Ed (Economics), B.Sc. Ed (Geography), B.Ed. Curriculum Studies, and various postgraduate programs.

Mass Communication

The Department of Mass Communication at Usmanu Danfodiyo University, Sokoto, was established in 2019. Commencing academic activities during the 2019/2020 session, the department focuses on producing professional and highly skilled Mass Communicators, journalists, and other media practitioners. The department offers programs in English and Literary Studies, French, History and International Studies, Linguistics, Mass Communication, and Nigerian Languages.

FACULTY OF ARTS IN BRIEF

The Faculty of Arts came into being in 1976, one of the pioneer faculties of the university. It started its academic activities with the creation of five departments, conducting wide-ranging research in the fields of Arts and Islamic Sciences.

BRIEF HISTORY OF THE DEPARTMENT

The French Section of the Department of Modern European Languages was established in the 1979/80 sessions. In the 1987/88 session, it gained autonomous status within the Department of Modern European Languages and has remained so to date.

BRIEF HISTORY OF THE DEPARTMENT

Forestry and Environment Department

The Department of Forestry and Environment at Usmanu Danfodiyo University, Sokoto, began its journey in the 1980/81 academic session as part of the University's comprehensive agricultural training, integrating Forestry and Fisheries. Initially, the University's agricultural curriculum stood out for incorporating these components, offering a distinctive approach compared to other institutions in the country. The National Universities Commission (NUC) introduced Approved Minimum Standards for Agriculture, Forestry, Fisheries, and Home Economics in 1989. Consequently, the University adopted the general Agriculture curriculum in the 1989/90 session, introducing two courses (one each in Forestry and Fisheries) at the 300 level. The old agriculture program concluded in the 1993/94 session.

Recognizing the vital role of Forestry and Fisheries in food production, especially in Northern Nigeria, the Senate approved undergraduate programs in Bachelor of Forestry (B. Forestry) and Bachelor of Fisheries (B. Fisheries) in April 1996. These programs officially commenced in the 1999/2000 academic session. In the last quarter of 2014, the department underwent bifurcation, resulting in two distinct programs: Forestry and Environment, and Fisheries and Aquaculture, effective August 2014.

Crop Science Department

The Department of Crop Science, established in 1975, stands as a significant entity within the Faculty of Agriculture at Usmanu Danfodiyo University. Offering a variety of courses at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels, the department focuses on providing tertiary education and research in crop production. Apart from delivering high-quality teaching, the department engages in advanced postgraduate research, encompassing fundamental and applied aspects, facilitated by modern laboratory facilities. Areas of expertise include Crop Anatomy and Physiology, Crop Genetics and Breeding, Weed Science, Entomology, Plant Pathology, Horticulture, and Irrigation Agronomy. The department operates well-equipped laboratories, three dedicated farms, and a screen house.

List of Academic Programs:

  • B. Agric. Crop Science
  • Master of Science
  • Doctor of Philosophy
  • Postgraduate Diploma Programme in Crop Production and Management (PGDCPM)

Soil Science and Agricultural Engineering Department

Established since the inception of Usmanu Danfodiyo University in 1975, the Department of Soil Science and Agricultural Engineering has been a cornerstone. Equipped with both theoretical and practical teaching resources, the department has produced qualified graduates contributing significantly to national development. With competent teaching and non-teaching staff, lecture theaters, laboratories, and research farms, the department has played a crucial role in training skilled professionals.

List of Academic Programs:

  • Bachelor of Agriculture (Soil/Crop option)
  • Master of Science in Soil Science
  • Master of Philosophy in Soil Science
  • Doctor of Philosophy in Soil Science
