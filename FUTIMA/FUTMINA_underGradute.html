Undergraduate Admission Information

Admission to undergraduate programs at the Federal University of Technology, Minna is exclusively through the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) and Direct Entry (DE). Only candidates who chose the university as their first choice during UTME or DE are eligible for the screening process.

UTME candidates must have achieved scores meeting the specified minimums for their chosen schools, courses, and programs, as outlined in the JAMB Brochure.

Candidates must also fulfill the minimum UTME scores for their selected courses and ensure the correct UTME subject combinations. Those failing to meet the requirements may switch to alternative courses, provided they meet the necessary criteria.

All applicants must possess a minimum of five 'O' Level credits, including Mathematics and English Language, relevant to their proposed courses, obtained in no more than two sittings. Direct Entry candidates should have a minimum of Lower Credit at National Diploma and/or Higher National Diploma levels or Merit at NCE level.

Postgraduate Admission Information

For admission into postgraduate programs, candidates must have obtained five credits, including English Language and Mathematics, in WAEC, NECO, NABTEB, or GCE ('O' Level), with no admission based on awaiting results. Ph.D. candidates must hold a Master's degree from the university or a recognized institution, maintaining a weighted score average of 60% or a 'B' grade or a CGPA of 3.50 out of 5.00 in their Master's program.

Ph.D. Program Duration: The Ph.D. program has a minimum duration of six semesters (three academic sessions) and a maximum duration of ten semesters (five academic sessions) for full-time students. Part-time students can complete it in a minimum of ten semesters (five academic sessions) and a maximum of fourteen semesters (seven academic sessions).

Master's Degree Admission Criteria: Master's degree candidates should have a minimum of Second Class Honours (Lower Division) or a Third Class Honours Degree with at least 3 years of graduation experience. HND holders with a minimum of Lower Credit, OND Lower Credit, and a Postgraduate Diploma with at least a Lower Credit and one year of graduation experience may be considered.

Admission Restrictions: Candidates with a PASS grade at any level of Degree, PGD, HND, and OND do not qualify for admission to Ph.D./Master/PGD programs. No Diploma candidate with a CGPA less than 2.50 qualifies for admission into Master's Degree programs.

Postgraduate Diploma Admission Criteria: Postgraduate Diploma candidates with a Third Class Honours Degree or HND with a minimum of Lower Credit and OND Lower Credit in relevant fields with at least a minimum of Lower Credit and one year of graduation experience may be considered.

Master Degree Program Duration: Master Degree programs are expected to last a minimum of three semesters for full-time students, with no part-time option. The maximum allowable period of studies is six semesters for full-time students, contingent on maintaining a CGPA of 2.50 in every semester. Failure to meet this requirement will result in withdrawal from the program.

Postgraduate Diploma Program Duration: Postgraduate Diploma programs typically last three semesters for full-time students, with the expectation of graduation on successful completion of the program, subject to maintaining a minimum CGPA of 1.50 in any semester.

The Federal University of Technology Minna (FUTMINNA) provides comprehensive information on undergraduate and postgraduate admissions, offering a diverse range of programs under the School of Agriculture and Agricultural Technology.

Postgraduate programs have strict admission standards, requiring a minimum of five credits in relevant subjects for Master's and Ph.D. candidates. The Ph.D. program spans six to ten semesters, while Master's programs are expected to last a minimum of three semesters.

Undergraduate Programs Admission Requirements

Admission into FUTMINNA's undergraduate programs is exclusively through the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) and Direct Entry (DE). Only candidates who select FUTMINNA as their first choice during UTME or DE are eligible for the screening exercise.

UTME candidates must score at least the indicated minimum scores for their chosen schools/courses/programs. Additionally, candidates must meet the minimum UTME scores for their selected courses and have the correct UTME subject combinations as outlined in the JAMB Brochure.

Candidates not meeting these requirements can change to alternative courses if they fulfill the criteria. All applicants must possess a minimum of five 'O' Level credits, including Mathematics and English Language, relevant to their proposed courses, obtained in no more than two sittings. Direct Entry candidates should have a minimum of Lower Credit at National Diploma and/or Higher National Diploma levels or Merit at NCE level.

UTME/O'Level Requirement into Degree Programs

Under the School of Agriculture and Agricultural Technology (SAAT), specific O'Level subject requirements and UTME combinations are outlined for various courses. These include Agricultural Economics and Farm Management, Agricultural Extension and Rural Development, Crop Production, Horticulture, Animal Production, Soil Science and Land Management, Water Resources, Aquaculture and Fisheries Technology, and Food Science Technology.

Postgraduate Programs Admission Requirements

Candidates applying for Postgraduate Studies at FUTMINNA must have obtained five credits in WAEC, NECO, NABTEB, or GCE ('O' Level) or equivalent, including English Language and Mathematics, in not more than two sittings. No admission will be based on awaiting 'O' Level results, and no exemptions are made.

Ph.D. Program Duration and Admission Criteria

Ph.D. candidates must be Master's degree graduates of FUTMINNA or any recognized university. A Master's degree holder applying for the Ph.D. program must have maintained a weighted score average of 60% or a 'B' grade or a CGPA of 3.50 out of 5.00 in the overall Master's program. The Ph.D. program's duration is a minimum of six semesters (three academic sessions) and a maximum of ten semesters (five academic sessions) for full-time students, with corresponding durations for part-time students.

Master's Degree Program Admission Criteria

For Master's degree admission, a minimum of Second Class Honours (Lower Division) is required, or a Third Class Honours Degree with at least 3 years of graduation experience may be considered. HND holders with a minimum of Lower Credit, OND Lower Credit, and a Postgraduate Diploma in a relevant field may also be considered.

Candidates with PASS grades at any level of Degree, PGD, HND, and OND, as well as those with a CGPA less than 2.50, do not qualify for admission into Master's Degree programs.

Postgraduate Diploma Program Admission Criteria

Postgraduate Diploma candidates with a Third Class Honours Degree or HND with a minimum of Lower Credit, OND Lower Credit, and one year of graduation experience may be considered.

Master Degree Program Duration

Master's degree programs are expected to last a minimum of three semesters for full-time students. There is no part-time option. The maximum allowable period of studies is six semesters for full-time students, contingent on maintaining a CGPA of 2.50 in every semester. Failure to meet this requirement will result in withdrawal from the program.

Postgraduate Diploma Program Duration

Candidates with PASS grades at either OND or HND do not qualify for admission into Postgraduate Diploma programs. Generally, full-time students are expected to graduate after three semesters on successful completion of their programs, subject to maintaining a minimum CGPA of 1.50 in any semester.
