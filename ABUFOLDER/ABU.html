Department of Mass Communication - Undergraduate Programme (B.Sc. Mass Communication)

The Department of Mass Communication at Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, Nigeria, provided the following information regarding the undergraduate program, B.Sc. (Mass Communication): According to the spokesperson, the program has a duration ranging from a minimum of 4 years to a maximum of 6 years for UTME/JAMB applicants and a minimum of 3 years to a maximum of 5 years for Direct Entry (D.E) candidates. For UTME Candidates, the entry requirements include obtaining five ‘O’Level Credits, comprising English Language, Mathematics, Government/History/Civic Education, Economics/Commerce/Financial Accounting, any Nigerian Language, and CRS/IRS. Additionally, candidates must pass Use of English, Government/History/Economics, and another subject at the UTME level. Direct Entry Candidates, on the other hand, should possess a Diploma in Mass Communication or its equivalent from A.B.U or any recognized University with a minimum of Merit Pass or IJMB of A.B.U in at least 3 Subjects. This should include minimum ‘C’ grade passes in Government/History, Economics, or any Nigerian Languages. Similar to UTME Candidates, they should also have five ‘O’Level Credits. The spokesperson emphasized that graduation requirements differ for UTME and Direct Entry Candidates. UTME Candidates are expected to complete their studies in a minimum of 4 years and a maximum of 6 years, accumulating a minimum of 120 credit units. Direct Entry Candidates, on the other hand, have a minimum of 3 years and a maximum of 5 years, with a minimum of 90 credit units. Department of Mass Communication allows students to register their courses in every semester for a minimum of 15 and a maximum of 24 credit units. Additionally, part of the graduation requirements includes participation in Students’ Industrial Training/SIWES, wherein all 400 level students must undergo an eight-week training in either print or broadcast media organizations for practical work experiences.

The Faculty seeks to advance and disseminate knowledge for the betterment of society and humanity, with a primary focus on nurturing graduates who not only find self-employment but also contribute significantly to national development.

To use education as a tool for societal transformation and positive change.

Ahmadu Bello University - Faculty of Engineering

Located in Zaria, Nigeria, Ahmadu Bello University is proud to host the Faculty of Engineering.

Led by the esteemed HOD, Prof. Mohammed-Dabo Ibrahim Ali, who also serves as the Dean of the Faculty, the institution aims to train high-level technical professionals with a vision to contribute to technological advancements both regionally and globally.

The Faculty has evolved from three to ten academic departments, offering diverse courses leading to Bachelor's, Master's, and Doctor of Philosophy degrees. The departments cover a wide range of engineering disciplines, fostering an environment that encourages students to leverage their ingenuity and creativity to address global challenges.

To meet contemporary needs, the Faculty introduced new undergraduate programs in Automotive and Mechatronics Engineering in the 2016/2017 session, with plans underway for the approval of the Industrial and Systems Engineering program. Prospective students are invited to be part of this pioneering initiative.

Collaborative efforts within the Faculty extend beyond disciplinary boundaries, with research teams addressing multidisciplinary challenges. Notably, the Faculty hosts two prestigious professorial chairs, the Shell and the Petroleum Technology Development Fund Chairs, contributing to groundbreaking research in the field of Oil and Gas.

Welcome Message:

The Faculty of Arts, situated at the heart of the Main Campus, Samaru, holds a distinguished position as one of the twelve Faculties of Ahmadu Bello University. With roots predating the formal establishment of the University, the Faculty played a pivotal role in the administrative and academic growth of the institution. The historical connection between the Faculty of Arts and Ahmadu Bello University traces back to the conception of a university in Northern Nigeria, following the recommendations of the Ashby Commission on Higher Education in Nigeria in 1959. The University, named after Sir Ahmadu Bello, came into existence on October 14, 1962, with Zaria chosen as its location due to the concentration of relevant academic institutions in the city.

MISSION:

To advance learning in the arts and humanities through teaching, research, and dissemination of knowledge of the highest quality. The mission also includes establishing national and international integration, developing and promoting African traditions and cultures, and producing high-level manpower to respond to community, national, and global challenges.

VISION:

The Faculty aspires to be a world-class institution, comparable to any other, engaged in imparting knowledge in the Arts and Humanities. It aims to achieve this using high-quality facilities and multidisciplinary approaches, promoting inclusivity without discrimination based on race, gender, or creed. The vision also emphasizes generating new ideas and intellectual practices relevant to the needs of the immediate community, Nigeria, and the world.

PHILOSOPHY:

As one of the foundational Faculties of Ahmadu Bello University, the Faculty of Arts has been integral to the University's administrative development and academic accomplishments. It continues to play a leading role in fostering a dynamic and enriching learning environment.
