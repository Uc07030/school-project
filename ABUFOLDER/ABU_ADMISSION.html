# Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria Nigeria Undergraduate Programme

B.Sc. (Mass Communication) Duration Minimum of 4 years and Maximum of 6years for UTME/JAMB and Minimum of 3 years and maximum of 5 years for Direct Entry (D.E)

Entry or Admission Requirements:

UTME Candidates Five ‘O’Level Credit in English Language, Mathematics, Government/History/Civic Education, Economics/Commerce/Financial Accounting, any Nigerian Language, and CRS/IRS plus Use of English, Government/History/Economics and any other subject at UTME level.

Entry Candidates

in Mass Communication from A.B.U or any recognized University with a minimum of Merit Pass or IJMB of A.B.U in at least 3 Subjects with minimum of ‘C’ grade passes which must include Government/History, Economics or any Nigerian Languages plus five ‘O’Level Credit in English Language, Mathematics, Government/History/Civic Education, Economics/Commerce/Financial Accounting, any Nigerian Language, and CRS/IRS.

Requirement - The UTME Candidates have a minimum of 4 years and a maximum of 6 years to graduate while the direct entry candidates have a minimum of 3 years and a maximum of 5 years to graduate. For the UTME, a minimum of 120 credit units must be passed before graduation, including the General Studies and Electives. In the case of direct entry, a minimum of 90 credit units must be passed before graduation, including the General Studies and Electives. Thus, in every semester, a minimum of 15 and maximum of 24 credit unit units can be registered for.

Ahmadu Bello University ABU library Kashim Ibrahim Library is an academic library that serves Ahmadu Bello University’s students and academic staff in Zaria, Kaduna. It is located on the university’s main campus, with satellite libraries distributed on various academic campuses. The library’s collection includes around 1.2 million books, 66000 titles of journals, online databases, a CD-ROM search room, and a Learning Commons. For successful services to the university community, the Library now contains over ten divisions.


Kashim Ibrahim Library was founded in 1955, the same year that the university was founded. It was then a modest room that was later transformed into a staff club. The little library was used until 1963, when a $39,000 modern library was built. The library was named after the state’s then-governor. The library has a capacity of 70000 books and can accommodate 120 people at any given moment. In 1963, the Governor, Sir Kashim Ibrahim, was honored with a library dedicated after him.
